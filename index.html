Ecology Researcher | Environmental Planner

My path into ecology began through art. Before I ever stepped into a field research role, I spent years drawing, photographing, and paying attention to the world around me. I was fascinated by small details, patterns, and the character of different landscapes. That habit of observation eventually led me toward ecology, where I became interested in understanding how species, habitats, and ecological communities change over time, and how those changes can inform restoration and conservation efforts.
