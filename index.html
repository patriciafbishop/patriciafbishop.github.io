Patricia Bishop

Environmental Planning · Ecology Research · Fieldwork

I’m a recent graduate of environmental planning and policy, ecology researcher, and artist based in Portland, Maine. My work is rooted in curiosity about wetlands, rivers, coastal habitats, and the species that move through them. I’m especially interested in restoration, fisheries ecology, habitat monitoring, and the ways science can be communicated through both research and visual storytelling.

Research Interests

  • Wetland ecology and coastal restoration
  • Fisheries ecology and anadromous fish habitat
  • Wildlife monitoring and habitat management
  • Climate adaptation and conservation planning
  • Science communication and visual storytelling

Research & Field Experience

My recent work includes juvenile alosine diet analysis in the Kennebec River system, biological sample processing in the USM Aquatic Systems Lab, and salt marsh restoration support at Rachel Carson National Wildlife Refuge.

Presentations

  • Diet Analysis of Juvenile Alewife and American Shad in Merrymeeting Bay — Thinking Matters Symposium, 2025
  • Juvenile Alosine Ecology in the Kennebec River — Maine Soil and Water Conference, 2025
